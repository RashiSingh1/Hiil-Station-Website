Coorg

Officially known as Kodagu, is a stunning, mountainous district in Karnataka, India, nestled in the Western Ghats and renowned as the "Scotland of India".

Top Attraction in Coorg

Talakaveri

Located on the Brahmagiri hills near Bhagamandala in Coorg (Kodagu, Karnataka), is the sacred birthplace of the River Kaveri. Situated around 44-48 km from Madikeri, this serene, 1,276-meter-high site features a small tank (Kundike).

Mandalpatti Viewpoint

A breathtaking 1,600–1,800 meter high peak in the Western Ghats known as "Muguli-peth" (market of clouds). It offers panoramic, 360-degree views of lush green hills, often with clouds at eye level, making it a top sunrise/sunset.

Nisargadhama

Kaveri Nisargadhama is a picturesque picnic spot known for its natural beauty and various activities. Visitors appreciate the serene environment and the opportunity to see wildlife, including deer and birds.

Namdroling Monastery

The Namdroling Monastery, popularly known as the Golden Temple in Bylakuppe near Coorg, is one of India's largest Tibetan Buddhist centers and home to the Nyingma lineage. Established in 1963, it features 40–60 foot gold-plated Buddha statues.

Mallalli Waterfalls

Mallalli Falls (or Mallalli Falls) is a stunning two-tiered, 200-foot waterfall in Coorg's Pushpagiri Hills, created by the Kumaradhara River. Best visited during the monsoon (July-September), it features powerful cascades, trekking paths.

Best Time To Visit

Summer (Mar–May)

  • Temperature: 20–35°C
  • Pleasent hill eather
  • Best for sightseeing and coffee platations tours
  • Perfect for trekking and outdoor activities

Monsson (Jun-Sep)

  • Temperature: 18–25°C
  • Heavy rainfall and misty hills
  • Waterfalls are at their most beautiful
  • Ideal for nature lovers and photographers

Post-Monsoon (Oct–Nov)

  • Temperature: 18–28°C
  • Fresh greenery after rains
  • Clear skies and scenic landscapes
  • Great time for exploring viewpoints

Winter (Dec–Feb)

  • Temperature: 10°C to 25°C
  • Perfect for peaceful vacations
  • Cool and comfortable weather
  • Ideal for trekking and plantation walks

Travel Tips

🌧

Rain is common in Coorg, especially during monsoon season. Carry an umbrella or raincoat while visiting waterfalls and forests.

Coorg is known for beautiful coffee plantations, misty hills, and waterfalls. Don’t forget your camera to capture the scenic landscapes.
