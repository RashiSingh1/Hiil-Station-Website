Shimla

Shimla, "Queen of Hill Stations," capital of Himachal Pradesh, is a premier North Indian hill station. Located on a 12km ridge in the Himalayas at an elevation of about 6,500 ft (approx. 2,200m).

Top Attraction in Shimla

Viceregal Lodge

Viceregal lodge is a historic, Scottish Baronial-style building in Shimla built in 1888 as the British Viceroy's summer residence. Located on Observatory Hill, it features extensive gardens and stone masonry, and currently houses the Indian Institute of Advanced Study.

Kalka-Shimla Railway

The Kalka-Shimla Railway is a 95.66 km (59.44 mi) narrow-gauge (2 ft 6 in) UNESCO World Heritage line in India, operating since 1903. Known as a “toy train” route, it connects Kalka (656 m) to the summer capital, Shimla (2076 m), featuring 103 tunnels

Christ Church

The historic college of the University of Oxford and its Cathedral, or various autonomous Christian congregations known as the Churches of Christ, which are rooted in the 19th-century American Restoration Movement.

Kurfi

Kufri is a popular, high-altitude hill station 20 km from Shimla in Himachal Pradesh, known for its cool climate, panoramic Himalayan views, and dense pine forests

Green Valley

Located near Kufri on the Shimla-Kufri road, is a renowned tourist stop in Himachal Pradesh, India, known for its stunning views of dense pine and deodar forests. It is a popular spot for photography

Travel Tips

🧥

Carry Warm Clothes

Weather in Shimla can be cool, especially in the evenings and winter months. Carry warm clothes or light jackets to stay comfortable.

👟

Wear Comfortable Shoes

Shimla has many walking areas like The Mall Road and hill viewpoints. Comfortable shoes will help you explore the town easily.

🚶

Start Early for Sightseeing

Popular places like Jakhoo Temple and Kufri can get crowded. Start your day early to enjoy a peaceful experience.

📷

Carry a Camera

Shimla offers beautiful mountain views, colonial buildings, and scenic landscapes. Don’t forget your camera to capture amazing moments.
