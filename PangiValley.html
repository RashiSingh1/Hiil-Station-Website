Top Attraction in Pangi Valley

Sach Pass

Sach Pass is a high-altitude mountain pass (4,425–4,550 m) in Himachal Pradesh's Chamba district, connecting the Churah Valley with the remote Pangi Valley. Known for its treacherous, unpaved roads and massive 15-foot snow walls

Chasak Bhatori

Chasak Bhatori is the highest, most remote, and only road-inaccessible Bhatori (high-altitude village) in Himachal Pradesh's Pangi Valley, located within the Sechu Tuan Nala Wildlife Sanctuary. Situated at 12,000 ft, it is a 6-8 km trek from Chasak village, offering views of the Singhmarh Dhar glacier, a Buddhist Gompa, and rare Himalayan wildlife.

Chandrabhaga River

The Chandrabhaga River flows through the entire 85-kilometer length of the Pangi Valley in Himachal Pradesh, acting as a crucial, fast-flowing water body. Running along the scenic Sansari-Killar-Thirot (SKT) road, it is widest near the villages of Phindroo and Phindpar, creating dramatic landscapes and river beaches.

Sural Bhatori

Sural Bhatori is a remote, high-altitude (3,500m) sub-valley in Himachal Pradesh's Pangi Valley, ideal for visiting from April to November. Renowned for its scenic beauty, it features the 300-year-old Gumpha monastery, Buddhist culture, and acts as a base camp for treks to Zanskar and the Chabi waterfall.

Hudan Bhatori

Hudan Bhatori, situated at 3200 meters in Himachal Pradesh's remote Pangi Valley, is a scenic village renowned for its mythological significance, traditional culture, and stunning landscape.

Best Time To Visit

Summer (May – June)

  • Weather becomes pleasant after winter snow melts.
  • Temperature: 10°C – 20°C
  • Roads start opening and travel becomes easier.
  • Perfect for sightseeing, trekking, and exploring villages.

Monsoon (July – September)

  • Valley becomes lush green and very scenic.
  • Rivers and waterfalls look beautiful.
  • Some roads can be slippery due to rain, so travel carefully.

Spring (March – April)

  • Snow begins to melt and the valley slowly turns green.
  • Weather remains cool but pleasant for travel.
  • A peaceful time with fewer tourists.
  • Perfect season for nature walks and photography.

Winter (October – February)

  • Heavy snowfall covers the entire valley.
  • Temperatures often fall below 0°C.
  • Many roads may close because of snow.
  • Suitable mainly for adventure lovers and snow experiences.

Travel Tips

🧥

Carry Warm Clothes

Pangi Valley has cold weather even in summer, especially at night. Carry warm jackets, gloves, and layers to stay comfortable.

⛽

Fuel Up & Carry Essentials

Petrol pumps and shops are limited in the valley. Fill your fuel tank in advance and carry basic supplies.

📶

Expect Limited Network

Mobile network and internet connectivity are weak in many areas. Inform family beforehand and keep offline maps ready.

🚗

Travel with a Reliable Vehicle

Roads to Pangi Valley are narrow and sometimes rough. Travel with a well-maintained vehicle and experienced driver.
