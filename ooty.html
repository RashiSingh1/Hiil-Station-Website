Ooty

Ooty (Udhagamandalam), the "Queen of Hill Stations" in Tamil Nadu, is a premier tourist destination in the Nilgiri Hills at 2,240 meters, known for its pleasant 12°C–30°C climate, tea gardens, and colonial charm

Top Attraction in Ooty

Doddabetta peak

Doddabetta Peak, located about 10 km from Ooty in Tamil Nadu, is the highest point in the Nilgiri Mountains at 2,637 meters ( feet). Known for its panoramic views, dense shola forests, and a telescope house, it is a popular, easily accessible tourist spot, particularly for sunrises and sunsets.

Pine Forest

Pine Forest is a serene, scenic spot featuring hundreds of tall, slender trees. Popularized by film shoots, it offers a quiet escape for photography, nature walks, and, in some areas, horse riding. It is easily accessible, approximately 9 km from Ooty.

Coonoor

The second-largest hill station in the Nilgiris, is a serene, tea-plantation-covered alternative to nearby Ooty, located in Tamil Nadu, India. Known for its milder climate, colonial charm, and attractions like Sim’s Park, Lamb's Rock, and the Nilgiri Mountain Railway

Ooty Lake

Ooty Lake is a popular 65-acre artificial, scenic lake in the Nilgiris district of Tamil Nadu, created by John Sullivan in 1824 for fishing. Located at an altitude of 2,240m

Avalanche Lake

Avalanche Lake in Ooty, Tamil Nadu, is a pristine, crystal-blue natural lake surrounded by lush Nilgiri forests and rolling hills, located about 20-30 km from Ooty.

Travel Tips

🧥

Carry Light Warm Clothes

Ooty weather can be cool, especially in mornings and evenings. Carry light jackets or sweaters for comfort.

💧

Stay Hydrated

While exploring gardens, hills, and viewpoints, keep drinking water to stay fresh and energized.

🌦

Check Weather Forecast

Weather in Ooty can change quickly, especially during monsoon. Check the forecast before planning outdoor activities.

👟

Wear Comfortable Shoes

Ooty has many walking trails, viewpoints, and gardens. Comfortable shoes will help you explore easily.
